Hi. I'm Morgan, and welcome to my personal career website. I am a computer scientist, entrepreneur, research scientist, data engineer, mathematician, and bold thinker. Read below to learn a bit about me..

In the last several years, I have been training in Silicon Valley to become a groundbreaking scientist, engineer, entrepreneur, and holistic 21st-century human. I am a currently finishing my Coterminal Master's degree in Symbolic Systems, with a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Science (conferred), both at Stanford University.

Over this period, I have been focusing on training my industry expertise in machine learning, software engineering, venture technology startups, and scientific research and development. As a Computer Science undergraduate I studied the principles, theory, engineering, and applications of software and computation. I further concentrated on statistics, artificial intelligence, and machine learning. My academic work since undergrad lies in the fields of psychology, neuroscience, computational science, and mathematics.

Most recently, I have been working on my Master's thesis with Stanford's PDP lab on distributed theories of human intelligence. To this end we use computational modeling and behavioral experiments as our core methodological tools. My primary research project focuses on how we learn, use, and conceptualize spatial reference frames to understand our environment, focusing especially on the roles of curricula, attention, navigation, and planning. Having finished the degree's coursework, I have taken the opportunity to finish my thesis remotely to be with my family in our hometown, Chicago. Since 2019, I have been also studying quantum physics, molecular biology, and macroeconomics in both theoretic and application-based directions. I am finding success and invigoration in using my applied mathematics, computer science, and artificial intelligence skills to these domains.

Today, I am seeking the best opportunities for my budding work career, intending to both develop and demonstrate my skills and interests in technology, startups, and research. As of August 2020, I am searching for a salaried full-time job as a data scientist at a tech startup in the Bay Area.

In the future, I am certainly going to complete a PhD program in Computational Neuroscience or a related field, starting no sooner than 2024. In the far future, I envision a career in which I evenly balance my efforts between industrial research science, academia, and the Silicon Valley tech startup industry. This combination of roles will make the best use of my specialized technical skillset, deep and broad knowledge of innovative, advanced technology, and my unrivaled knack for synthesizing and integrating methods in unprecedented ways, in order to pursue my goal of solving the world's most challenging and impactful problems in neuroscience, data science, biotechnology, and justice.

Outside of work, I focus my resources on art (especially sound art and experimental music, installation and performance art, and interactive digital and text media) and social good and intersectional justice (especially LGBT rights, racial and socioeconomic justice, and mental health transparency).
